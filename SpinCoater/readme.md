# Spin Coater Procurement: Handover Document

This document summarizes the research and evaluation of spin coaters for **Jing's Lab** (shipping to MIT). It is intended to serve as a handover for other researchers taking over the procurement process. 

* **Original Discussion Thread:** [GitHub Issue #1: Finding Spin Coater](https://github.com/MarioTaiwan/NMElab/issues/1)
* **Total Budget:** $6,000.00 USD

---

## 1. Specifications Checklist

To meet the lab's experimental needs, the spin coater must satisfy the following criteria:

### Minimum Requirements (Must-Haves)
* **Two-Zone Program:** At least a 2-stage programmable process.
* **Thickness Precision:** High-precision control for film thickness (specifically spin-coating PMMA A4 or A9 on mostly 1-inch substrates).
* **Vacuum Chuck:** Must include or support a vacuum chuck.
* **Reproducibility & Robustness:** Highly valued for 2D material research.

### Nice-to-Have Features
* **Automated Dispenser:** For controlled, hands-free chemical deposition.
* **Heater/Baking Stage:** Integrated heating element.

## 3. Detailed Breakdown of Options

### Option 1: Laboratory Programmable Compact Spin Coater EZ4C

* **Cons:** Touch screen interface; vacuum pump is sold separately; advanced add-ons are cost-prohibitive.

* **Price:** **$6,624.00 USD** (Special negotiated price)

### Option 7: Specialty Coating Systems (SCS) 6808P
* **Price:** **$6,250.00+ USD**

## 4. Key Procurement Takeaways & Recommendations

1. **The Heating Cover Retrofit (Action Required):**
   MTI is willing to sell us just a heating cover to upgrade an existing spin coater. *Recommendation: Find the manufacturer tag on the lab's current spin coater and send it to Mitchie Li at MTI to see if this cheap retrofit is possible.*
2. **If Integrated Heating is Required (Option 6):**
   If a retrofit fails, the **Aurora ProSci with Heating Stage ($6,624.00)** is our cheapest path to a heated spin coater. It exceeds the budget by $624.00 and requires us to supply our own vacuum pump, but is vastly cheaper than MTI's quoted full heated systems.
3. **If Absolute Precision & Standard Reliability are Crucial (Option 7):**
   The **SCS 6808P ($6,250.00+)** is an industry benchmark for precision 2D material/PMMA workflows. It slightly exceeds our budget and requires us to buy chucks and a pump separately, but offers legendary hardware longevity. 
4. **If Dispensing Automation is Priority (Option 1):**
   If automation is critical and we cannot expand our budget, the **EZ4C ($2,028.98)** is a highly economical choice that includes both a pump and a dispenser cover, leaving plenty of budget room to adapt our own automated fluid dispenser system
